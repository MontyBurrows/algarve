Friday's mood

The calm night — port or wine.

Pick one tasting venue, then a proper Portuguese dinner with a bottle. No need to do both.

Oporto Tavern
Wine & cocktail bar · Vilamoura marina · 17:00–02:00
Port option
The port answer. Bruno and the team run an informal port flight at the bar — they line up 3–5 ports based on what you like, talk through each one, pair with tapas cooked on the bar in some sittings. Weekday happy hour (17:00–19:30) is the quietest window with most bartender attention. Open till 02:00 if you want to stay.
DM to book Reviews Maps
Rolha a Rolha
Wine shop & tastings · Quarteira (Sá Carneiro area) · 5 min from apartment
Wine option
The wine alternative. A newer Quarteira wine shop running structured two-hour Portuguese wine tastings — seven wines, walked through by variety and region. More formal sit-down vibe than Oporto Tavern, and you don't have to leave Quarteira. Book a day or two ahead.

Saturday's mood

The lively night — bars, music, dance.

A marina full of options. Start at Atlantic, see how the night goes — bar-hop, escalate to a club, or take the wildcard boat-party route.

Atlantic Bar
Vilamoura marina · Live music + DJ · Year-round · Open till 02:00
Base camp
The reliable opener. Live music venue first — bands from across Portugal, UK guests, transitions to live DJ later. Marina-side, mixed-but-younger crowd, no aggressive door. Cocktails around €8–10. White baby grand piano in the corner, energetic without being a club. Good place to start the night and feel out the vibe.
Reviews Maps
If you change your mind
Marina bar-hop alternatives · Young, music-led, all walkable from Atlantic
Mix & match
Three more marina bars worth knowing about, all within a few minutes of Atlantic. Drop in for one drink each, find the one with the right energy on the night, settle in there.
Cats Bar
Av. da Marina 22 · Live DJ sets · Lively crowd
Marina nightspot with regular DJs on weekends, casual-stylish crowd, marina views. Arrive a bit early for a seat. House cocktails are the order to make.
MöTAO Vilamoura
Marina · Trendy · Asian-inspired bar & lounge
Newer Vilamoura entrant — stylish décor, beach atmosphere, indoor and outdoor zones, attracts a younger and trendier crowd than the older Irish pubs. Good cocktails, lounge vibe.
O'Neills Irish Pub
Marina front · Live music every night · Marina terrace
If the brief is "live band, energetic crowd, decent pints" — this is the simple answer. Live music every night, marina-overlooking terrace, pool table inside, lively without being a nightclub. Gets crowded; service can slow.
Kadoc Disco
Estrada de Vilamoura, Boliqueime · 10-min taxi · Year-round · Till 07:00
Late escalation
The Algarve's biggest superclub — 7,000 capacity, five dance floors, open-air terrace, year-round (Bliss is summer-only, Lick has bad reviews). Mixed-age crowd, less attitude than the smaller clubs. €10–15 entry plus taxi. Best as a 01:00+ escalation after a couple of marina bars, not first stop.
Maps
Sunset Boat Party
Vilamoura marina · 2 hours · Open bar first hour · €35–45 each
Wildcard
A 2-hour boat party along the coast at sunset — live DJ, open bar first hour (beer, sangria, soft drinks), dance deck. Solves the "no real club in late May" question by making the boat the party. Departs Vilamoura ~18:00, back ~20:00. Then dinner, then Atlantic Bar to keep it going. Book ahead via GetYourGuide or SeaBookings.

What we're skipping: Bliss is closed (July–August only). Lick gets aggressive bouncer / €5-water-bottle reviews. Disco Dice has sticky floors. Stick to Atlantic Bar plus a marina bar-hop, with Kadoc as the late option or the boat party as a wildcard.
Daytime moves

Easy walks & the daytime.

Gentle stuff only — the proper hiking trip's coming later.

Praia da Falésia clifftop walk
From Vilamoura marina · Free · 60–90 min there-and-back
Easy walk
Iconic Algarve orange cliffs with pine trees. Start near the eastern end of Praia da Rocha Baixinha. Hour out, hour back — gentle clifftop path with viewpoints all along. Trainers, water, sunscreen. Cliff edges crumble; stay back from the lip.
Trail start
Cerro da Vila Roman Ruins
Near Vilamoura marina · €4 each · Mon–Fri 09:30–12:30 / 14:00–18:00
Friday only
A real Roman villa with mosaics, fish-salting tanks and a small museum. Walking distance from the marina. Closed Saturday and Sunday — Friday is your only window on this trip. Allow 60–90 minutes. Properly interesting culture hit for €4.
Maps
Loulé Saturday Market
Mercado Municipal · Saturday only · Bus 9 from Quarteira
Saturday morning
The famous Moorish red-domed market hall with farmers' and gypsy markets spilling around it on Saturdays. Live music inside the hall. Petiscos at the stalls for lunch (~€10–12). Loulé castle and old town wander combine perfectly. Bus 9 from Quarteira, ~25 min, €3.50 each via Vamus app.

Quarteira seafront promenade
Calçadão de Quarteira · Free · Flat 2km between apartment and marina
Easy walk
The Calçadão. Palm-lined paved promenade between your apartment and Vilamoura marina — easy, flat, scenic. At the eastern end: Quarteira's working fish port and morning fish market, gritty and authentic and free.

Where to eat

Three dinners, three moods.

Thursday's easy, Friday's traditional, Saturday's modern shared plates. Plus the market lunch in Loulé.

Casa do Pescador
Just behind Vilamoura marina · Mains €15–25 · Traditional Portuguese
Friday night
Friday's dinner. Traditional seafood and steak, hidden square behind the marina, generous portions. Famous for cataplana — a copper-pot Portuguese fish stew you split between two. Book ahead for Friday/Saturday. ~€30–40 each with a bottle of wine.
Find on Maps Reviews
Salmora Live Kitchen & Bar
Just off marina · Sharing menu from €6.75 · Modern Portuguese tapas
Saturday night
Saturday's dinner. Modern Portuguese tapas, open kitchen, social bar seating — perfect for two splitting plates. Order six to eight small plates between you (mushroom risotto, gyozas, baby squid, mini-burgers). ~€30–40 each with cocktails. Book ahead for Saturday.
Find on Maps
Akvavit
Vilamoura marina-front · Mains €18–29 · Scandi-Portuguese since 1990
Alternative
Iconic marina-front restaurant. Swedish-Portuguese fusion (grilled tiger prawns, cataplana, lamb chops). Marina-deck terrace, good Portuguese wine list. Smart-casual. Swap into Friday or Saturday if Casa do Pescador or Salmora is fully booked.

Quarteira backstreets
For Thursday's dinner and Sunday lunch
Local
The backstreets a few rows behind the seafront have the best-value Portuguese cooking. Restaurante Tico Tico for traditional family-run dinner — Thursday's pick. Sailor's Corner for the lighter Sunday lunch with a final beer. Rei do Churrasco for Madeiran-style grilled chicken if you want something quick. Marisqueira Sol e Mar for local fish — ask the price per kilo before ordering.
